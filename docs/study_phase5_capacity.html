Airtime Math — Time-on-Air per MCS × Frame Size + Overheads

Prereq: Phase 1 Day 1–2 (OFDM, MCS); Phase 2 Day 1 (EDCA)
Socratic Questions
30 clients each push 2 Mbps at MCS 5 (52 Mbps PHY rate) on 40 MHz. Using the airtime formula: ToA = (frame_size / PHY_rate) + SIFS + ACK_duration + DIFS + backoff. What's the projected channel utilization — and at what client count does it exceed 80%?
The Rev Wi-Fi planner uses "medium time" as the primary capacity unit. Why is medium time a better capacity metric than throughput in Mbps — especially in a mixed MCS environment?

RF Coverage Tiers — −67/−72/−75 dBm, RSSI Asymmetry

Prereq: Phase 1 Day 2 (MCS/SNR floor)
Socratic Questions
WLPC requires −67 dBm as the primary coverage tier for voice. Why is the design RSSI asymmetric between uplink and downlink — and which direction is the constraint when the AP has 4 antennas and the iPhone has 2?
The Rev Wi-Fi planner sets a "minimum MCS gate" — it won't count a client toward capacity if their RSSI puts them below a minimum MCS. Why is this gate necessary, and what happens to cell throughput when you remove it?

SSID Overhead — Beacon Airtime Cost per SSID per Band

Prereq: Day 1 (airtime math)
Socratic Questions
Each SSID adds one beacon per beacon interval per band. At a 100 TU beacon interval with 4 SSIDs, and assuming beacons are sent at the lowest mandatory rate (6 Mbps on 5 GHz), what's the beacon-only airtime overhead as a percentage of total medium time — and why does this compound with legacy client rates?
The SSID Overhead Calculator shows that adding a 4th SSID costs ~8% airtime even with zero clients. Why does the overhead increase linearly with SSID count rather than staying flat?

Co-Channel Contention vs Adjacent-Channel Interference

Prereq: Phase 1 Day 3 (channel widths, primary/secondary); Day 1 (airtime)
Socratic Questions
"The Fallacy of Channel Overlap" argues 2 APs on the same channel is better than 2 APs on adjacent channels in a dense reuse pattern. Why — and at what inter-AP distance does the equation flip?
Co-channel contention causes CSMA deferral — APs hear each other and take turns. Adjacent-channel interference causes MAC-layer collisions that the receiver can't decode. Which one is more damaging to voice traffic quality — and why?
Reading (T1 Corpus)

The Fallacy of Channel Overlap.pdf (×2 in corpus — both versions)

Lab Exercise
CCA Deferral Observation
If a second test radio is available: place both APs on the same channel 5m apart → AP1 capture → observe CCA deferral events in Wireshark via show wifi channel-utilization on AP1 → measure retry rate vs adjacent-channel scenario (change to ch 36/40) → compare.

Wi-Fi 7 Capacity Model — MLO + Multi-RU + 320 MHz Ceiling

Prereq: Phase 1 Day 8 (Wi-Fi 7 PHY); Day 1–2 (airtime, RSSI tiers)
Socratic Questions
320 MHz channels only exist in 6 GHz. An iPhone 15 has 2 spatial streams and supports MLO STR. In a 30-client meeting room, what's the realistic per-client throughput ceiling — and how does it change when 20 of the 30 clients are iPhone 14 (no MLO)?
The Rev Wi-Fi planner v2 has a Wi-Fi 7 sheet. It models EMLSR separately from STR. When does MLO STR provide throughput gain, when does it provide latency reduction, and when does it provide neither?
