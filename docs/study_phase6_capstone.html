WLPC 2026 Six-Process Framework

Prereq: All phases 1–5 complete
Manage

Scope, stakeholders, SLA definition, change window

Investigate

Symptoms, history, topology, known-good baseline

Measure

RSSI, SNR, retry %, channel utilization, DHCP timing

Validate

Compare measured vs expected; confirm scope of impact

Analyze

Root cause from evidence; eliminate alternates systematically

Interrogate

Packet capture, decode, frame-level proof of hypothesis

Socratic Questions
In the May 6 GTK rotation cascade incident: which of the six WLPC processes was skipped on the first attempt — and which process was the one that finally produced the ground-truth evidence that confirmed the root cause?
The WLPC framework puts "Interrogate" last. Why is running a packet capture as the first step actually counterproductive — and what should you have from the "Measure" stage before opening Wireshark?

Frame Stats Interpretation — Retries, BAR, Retry % Thresholds

Prereq: Phase 2 Day 3 (A-MPDU, BlockAck); Day 1–2 (WLPC framework)
Socratic Questions
AP1 show wifi stats shows 18% retry rate for one client but 4% cell-wide. What are the 3 most likely per-client causes — and which single Wireshark filter lets you confirm the dominant one in under 2 minutes?
A BAR (BlockAck Request) without a preceding burst of data frames is a red flag. What sequence of events forces an AP to send a standalone BAR — and what does it tell you about the client's receive-side state?
